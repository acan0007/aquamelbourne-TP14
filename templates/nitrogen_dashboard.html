Learn About Nitrogen Pollution

WHAT

Nitrogen pollution primarily refers to the contamination of rivers, lakes, and coastal areas with excess nitrogen compounds, such as nitrates (NO3âˆ’) and ammonium (NH4+), typically from human activities like agriculture, urban runoff, and wastewater discharge. Nitrogen pollution in water can have severe environmental impacts, particularly when it leads to eutrophication.

So What Happens Because of Nitrogen Pollution

Eutrophication and Algal Blooms

Excess nitrogen from fertilizers, wastewater, and other sources enters waterways and causes nutrient overload.

This leads to eutrophication, where rapid algae growth depletes oxygen in the water, creating dead zones that harm aquatic life.

Degraded Water Quality

High levels of nitrogen in waterways result in poor water quality, impacting ecosystems and biodiversity.

Toxic algal blooms caused by nitrogen runoff can contaminate water sources, making them unsafe for human and animal consumption.

Groundwater Contamination

Nitrate, a form of nitrogen, can seep into groundwater supplies, posing serious health risks, including blue baby syndrome in infants and an increased risk of cancer in adults.
