Ever Wondered Why Stormwater Pits Matter?

Introduction

Stormwater pits, also known as catch basins or stormwater sumps, are structures designed to collect and manage rainwater runoff from streets, roofs, and other impervious surfaces.
They are an essential part of urban drainage systems and help prevent flooding, soil erosion, and water pollution by directing stormwater away from populated areas and into natural water bodies or treatment facilities.

Melbourne's stormwater system includes over 300,000 stormwater pits that help manage rainwater runoff across the city.

How to Use the Tool

Once the map loads, you can zoom in or out and drag the map to explore different areas of Melbourne. Click on any marker to view detailed information about the specific pit, including its condition, maintenance status, and risk level. The map includes a legend to help you understand the different symbols and colors.

Educational Content Integration

Flushing Toilets

Stormwater Systems are designed to manage rainfall and surface runoff. When it rains, stormwater flows over streets, rooftops, and other hard surfaces, collecting pollutants along the way.

The stormwater system captures this runoff through gutters and drains, channeling it to stormwater basins or natural water bodies. The primary goal is to prevent flooding and manage water flow, not to treat or handle waste.

Irrigation

In Melbourne, stormwater can be effectively captured and utilized for irrigation in both urban and residential areas through practices such as rainwater harvesting systems.

These systems allow residents and businesses to collect and store rainwater for use in maintaining gardens, parks, and green spaces, reducing the demand on potable water supplies.

What is a Stormwater Drainage System? Stormwater Drainage Design

Rainfall results in the formation of stormwater. Stormwater that is not absorbed by the ground due to impermeable pavement or hard surface, spills over to the road or hard surface, and is collected by the underground drainage system, called stormwater drainage system.
